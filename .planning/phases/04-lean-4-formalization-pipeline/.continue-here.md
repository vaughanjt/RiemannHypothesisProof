- The remainder R(t) = O(t^{-1/4}) contributes +0.08
- At large T, R(t)/Z(t) -> 0, so r_RS -> r_0

### Attack plan
1. **Bound the remainder's effect on r**: R(t) = O(t^{-1/4}) with known leading
   coefficient. Its effect on the gap-peak correlation can be bounded by
   analyzing how a small additive perturbation to Z affects its zeros and peaks.

2. **Compute r for the truncated RS sum analytically**: This is a finite cosine
   sum f(t) = 2 * sum_{n=1}^{N(t)} cos(theta(t) - t*log(n))/sqrt(n).
   The gap-peak correlation depends on the interference pattern of these cosines.
   This is a well-posed harmonic analysis problem.

3. **The decomposition**: r = r_smooth + r_interference
   - r_smooth comes from theta'(t) variation (accounts for ~0.34 of gap prediction)
   - r_interference comes from cosine beating (accounts for the rest)
   - Both are deterministic functions of the RS sum parameters
