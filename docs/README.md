# Riemann Research Papers

Computational research papers on the fine structure of Riemann zeta zero statistics, produced as part of the [Riemann](../) hybrid computational research platform.

## Papers

### Paper 1: Quantitative Structure of the Finite-*T* Pair Correlation Correction for Riemann Zeta Zeros

**File:** `finite_T_pair_correlation.tex`

Characterizes the deviation of zeta zero pair correlation from the GUE random matrix prediction at finite observation height *T*. Using 10,000 Odlyzko zeros near *T* ~ 2.68 x 10^11, we decompose the autocorrelation excess into two components -- an oscillatory prime-frequency sum and a short-range repulsion correction -- and prove by Monte Carlo simulation that no third component exists.

**Key results:**
- Two-component decomposition: oscillatory (prime cosines, ~70%) + short-range (enhanced repulsion, ~30%)
- Correct finite-*T* amplitude law is log^2 *p* / *p* (Bogomolny-Keating), not log *p* / *p* (Selberg/Montgomery)
- Higher-order correlations (*k* >= 3) are indistinguishable from GUE -- arithmetic modulation is pair-correlation exclusive
- Five constraints on any candidate Hilbert-Polya operator

### Paper 2: Eigenvector Rigidity of Riemann Zeta Zeros -- A Structural Departure from Random Matrix Theory

**File:** `eigenvector_rigidity.tex`

Reports that the Riemann-Siegel *Z* function at the midpoint between consecutive zeta zeros is correlated with the zero gap at Pearson *r* = +0.75 (*T* ~ 458) to *r* = +0.80 (*T* ~ 2.7 x 10^11), compared to *r* = +0.04 for GUE random matrices -- a 20-fold excess.

**Key results:**
- Peak-gap correlation ("eigenvector rigidity") is 17-20x the GUE baseline
- The coupling is structural: 91% from the first five Riemann-Siegel terms
- The constant phase -pi/8 = arg Gamma(1/4) is the measurable fingerprint of the functional equation (+0.029 to *r*)
- Direct prime modulation of wave function amplitude detected at Bonferroni-corrected significance
- Sixth constraint on the Hilbert-Polya operator: eigenvectors cannot be Haar/independent

### Paper 3: Spectral Evidence for the Nyman-Beurling Criterion -- Stabilization of the Overlap Exponent gamma

**File:** `gamma_preprint.tex`

Systematic spectral decomposition of the Nyman-Beurling approximation problem. Computes the Gram matrix, its eigendecomposition, and the spectral projection for basis sizes up to N = 20,000.

**Key results:**
- Overlap exponent gamma stabilizes at ~1.91 (RH prediction is gamma = 2)
- 96% of d_N^2 reduction from prime-indexed basis functions
- Galois classification predicts per-prime contributions with *r* = +0.82
- Number-Theoretic Uncertainty Principle: if proved, implies RH
- SVD identity reduces RH to a bilinear exponential sum bound
- Extension to chi_4: C(k) = +/-1/2 identity proved, RKHS norm ||1/k||^2 = 2 exactly

### Paper 4: Gauge Symmetry, Arithmetic Modes, and Analytic Continuation -- Toward the Hilbert-Polya Operator via the Explicit Formula

**File:** `operator_framework.tex`

Systematic exploration of operator constructions for the Hilbert-Polya conjecture. Proves a gauge symmetry theorem eliminating Hermitian transfer operators, constructs an explicit formula Jacobi operator with arithmetic mode decomposition, and demonstrates analytic continuation to the critical line.

**Key results:**
- Gauge symmetry theorem: Hermitian transfer operators with conjugate phases have t-independent spectra (proven)
- Explicit formula Jacobi operator places 67% of eigenvalues within half a mean spacing
- Inert/split dichotomy: primes 3 mod 8 dominate coupling (C = 3.47), primes 5 mod 8 are silent (C < 0.001)
- Ramanujan sum decomposition of explicit formula error: q=1 coefficient = 7/8
- Analytic continuation yields smooth eigenvalue trajectories (eig_1: 17.81 -> 14.18, zero at 14.13)
- Spectral completeness identified as the remaining obstacle

## Building

Compile with any standard LaTeX distribution:

```bash
pdflatex finite_T_pair_correlation.tex
pdflatex eigenvector_rigidity.tex
pdflatex gamma_preprint.tex
pdflatex operator_framework.tex
```

Figure PDFs are generated by scripts in this directory:
- `make_paper1_figures.py` -- ACF, amplitude, and harmonics figures for Paper 1
- `make_figures.py` -- scatter plot and phase hierarchy figures for Paper 2
