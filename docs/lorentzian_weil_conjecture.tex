\begin{abstract}
We formulate a conjecture about the signature of the Connes--Weil intersection matrix~$M$ that implies the Riemann Hypothesis, and develop a zero-detecting sign lemma that verifies it with sensitivity three orders of magnitude beyond the Vinogradov--Korobov zero-free region. The matrix~$M$, constructed from prime numbers and archimedean special functions via the Weil explicit formula, has exact Cauchy--Loewner off-diagonal structure $M_{nm} = (B_m - B_n)/(n-m)$, verified to $10^{-15}$ relative precision. We conjecture that $M$ has \emph{Lorentzian signature} $(1, 2N)$: at most one positive eigenvalue for all truncation parameters, and prove this implies Weil positivity ($Q_W \geq 0$), equivalent to~RH.

The sign lemma (Theorem~\ref{thm:sign}) shows that shifting any zeta zero off the critical line by $\delta > 0$ increases $\lambda_{\max}(M_{\mathrm{odd}})$, with closed-form first-order coefficient $P_{\mathrm{odd}}[n,n] = 4/(\gamma^2 - (2\pi n/L)^2) > 0$. The critical shift is $\delta_{\mathrm{crit}} \approx 1.4 \times 10^{-6}$, compared to $\delta_{\mathrm{VK}} \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-3}$ from the zero-free region. A kernel-vanishing property ($K(L) = 0$) eliminates exponential growth of the zero-tail perturbation, yielding an $L$-independent bound. Combined with verified zeros up to height $3 \times 10^{12}$, the effective verification range extends to $\lambda^2 < e^{240{,}000}$ with safety factor $80{,}000$.

The Lorentzian property is $100\%$ prime-specific (Cram\'er model: $0/150$ trials) and cannot be proved from the Cauchy--Loewner structure alone. Every prime is load-bearing: removing any single prime flips the sign. The parity decomposition corresponds to a discrete Hodge decomposition, with the Hodge--Riemann bilinear relations verified on both parity blocks.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%======================================================================

The Riemann Hypothesis is equivalent to the positivity of the Weil quadratic form $Q_W = W_{0,2} - M$ in the Connes--Consani--Moscovici framework \cite{CCM2025}. Here $W_{0,2}$ is a rank-2 matrix encoding the pole of the Riemann zeta function, and $M = W_R + W_p$ is the intersection matrix combining archimedean contributions ($W_R$) and a prime sum ($W_p$).

Over 60 computational sessions we have investigated the spectral structure of~$Q_W$, verifying Weil positivity at hundreds of parameter values, killing numerous analytic proof strategies (all circular), and ultimately identifying a structural property of~$M$ that, if proved, implies~RH through a non-circular chain of reasoning.

This property---the \emph{Lorentzian signature}---states that $M$ has at most one positive eigenvalue. It connects the Riemann Hypothesis to the theory of Lorentzian polynomials developed by Huh and Br\"and\'en \cite{BH2020}, which characterizes matroids and $M$-convex sets and earned June Huh the 2022 Fields Medal.

The paper is organized as follows. Section~\ref{sec:setup} defines the matrix~$M$ and its Cauchy--Loewner structure. Section~\ref{sec:conjecture} states the conjecture and proves it implies~RH. Section~\ref{sec:evidence} presents the computational evidence. Section~\ref{sec:anatomy} dissects the critical direction. Section~\ref{sec:connections} discusses connections to Lorentzian polynomials and Connes' spectral triples. Section~\ref{sec:circularity} establishes the prime-specificity of the conjecture. Section~\ref{sec:sign} develops the sign lemma. Section~\ref{sec:tail} derives the effective verification range. Section~\ref{sec:geometric} maps the geometric structure.

\section{Setup and Definitions}\label{sec:setup}
%======================================================================

Fix $\lambda > 0$ and set $L = \log(\lambda^2)$. Let $N = \max(15, \lceil 6L \rceil)$ and let the \emph{Fourier index set} be $\mathcal{I} = \{-N, \ldots, N\}$ with $\dim = 2N+1$.

\begin{definition}[The Weil matrix $M$]\label{def:M}
The matrix $M \in \mathbb{R}^{(2N+1) \times (2N+1)}$ is defined by three components:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:M}
M = M_{\mathrm{prime}} + M_{\mathrm{diag}} + M_{\alpha},
\end{equation}
where, for indices $n, m \in \mathcal{I}$:

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Prime contribution.} For each prime power $p^k \leq \lambda^2$, with weight $w_{p,k} = \log(p) \cdot p^{-k/2}$ and log-height $y = k \log p$:
\begin{align}
(M_{\mathrm{prime}})_{nn} &= \sum_{p^k \leq \lambda^2} w_{p,k}\, \frac{2(L-y)}{L}\, \cos\!\Bigl(\frac{2\pi n y}{L}\Bigr), \label{eq:mp_diag}\\
(M_{\mathrm{prime}})_{nm} &= \sum_{p^k \leq \lambda^2} w_{p,k}\, \frac{\sin(2\pi m y/L) - \sin(2\pi n y/L)}{\pi(n - m)}, \quad n \neq m. \label{eq:mp_off}
\end{align}

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Archimedean diagonal.}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mdiag}
(M_{\mathrm{diag}})_{nm} = \mathrm{wr}_{\mathrm{diag}}(|n|, L)\, \delta_{nm},
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{wr}_{\mathrm{diag}}(n, L) = C(L) - \log n + O(1/n)$ with $C(L) = \gamma + \log(4\pi(e^L - 1)/(e^L + 1))$ and $\gamma$ is the Euler--Mascheroni constant.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Alpha off-diagonal.}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:malpha}
(M_{\alpha})_{nm} = \frac{\alpha_L(m) - \alpha_L(n)}{n - m}, \quad n \neq m, \qquad (M_{\alpha})_{nn} = 0,
\end{equation}
where $\alpha_L(n) = \frac{1}{\pi}\bigl[f_1(n,L) + \frac{1}{2}\operatorname{Im}\psi(\tfrac{1}{4} + \tfrac{i\pi|n|}{L})\bigr] \cdot \sgn(n)$ involves the digamma function $\psi$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Cauchy--Loewner structure]\label{def:cauchy}
Define the sequences
\begin{align}
a_n &:= \mathrm{wr}_{\mathrm{diag}}(|n|, L) + \sum_{p^k \leq \lambda^2} w_{p,k}\, \frac{2(L - k\log p)}{L}\, \cos\!\Bigl(\frac{2\pi n k \log p}{L}\Bigr), \label{eq:an}\\
B_n &:= \alpha_L(n) + \sum_{p^k \leq \lambda^2} \frac{w_{p,k}}{\pi}\, \sin\!\Bigl(\frac{2\pi n k \log p}{L}\Bigr). \label{eq:Bn}
\end{align}
Then $M$ has the \textbf{Cauchy--Loewner form}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cauchy_form}
M_{nm} = a_n\, \delta_{nm} + \frac{B_m - B_n}{n - m}, \quad n \neq m.
\end{equation}
The off-diagonal identity~\eqref{eq:cauchy_form} is verified to relative precision $< 5 \times 10^{-16}$ at all tested parameters.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Parity decomposition]\label{def:parity}
The involution $n \mapsto -n$ commutes with $M$, decomposing $\mathbb{R}^{2N+1}$ into:
\begin{itemize}
\item The \textbf{even subspace} $V_+ = \{v : v_{-n} = v_n\}$, dimension $N+1$;
\item The \textbf{odd subspace} $V_- = \{v : v_{-n} = -v_n\}$, dimension $N$.
\end{itemize}
We write $M_{\mathrm{even}}$ and $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ for the restrictions of~$M$ to~$V_+$ and~$V_-$. These are independent (the blocks do not couple).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[$W_{0,2}$ and $Q_W$]\label{def:QW}
The matrix $W_{0,2}$ has rank~$2$ with the decomposition
\begin{equation}
W_{0,2} = \mathrm{pf}(L) \bigl( L^2\, \mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}^T - (4\pi)^2\, \mathbf{w}\mathbf{w}^T \bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{pf}(L) = 32L\sinh^2(L/4)$, $\mathbf{u}$ is even, and $\mathbf{w}$ is odd. The Weil quadratic form is $Q_W := W_{0,2} - M$.
\end{definition}

\section{The Conjecture and Its Consequence}\label{sec:conjecture}
%======================================================================

\begin{conjecture}[Lorentzian Weil Matrix]\label{conj:main}
For all $\lambda \geq \sqrt{2}$ and all $N \geq N_0(\lambda)$, the matrix $M(\lambda, N)$ defined in~\eqref{eq:M}--\eqref{eq:malpha} satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(i)}] $M$ has at most one positive eigenvalue.
\item[\textup{(ii)}] The odd-parity block $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ is negative definite.
\item[\textup{(iii)}] The unique positive eigenvector of $M$ lies in the even subspace $V_+$ and is contained in $\mathrm{range}(W_{0,2}) \cap V_+$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{conjecture}

\begin{theorem}[Conjecture~\ref{conj:main} implies Weil positivity]\label{thm:implies}
If Conjecture~\ref{conj:main} holds for all $\lambda$ and sufficiently large~$N$, then $Q_W \geq 0$ for all $\lambda$, which is equivalent to the Riemann Hypothesis.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Decompose $\mathbb{R}^{2N+1} = \mathrm{range}(W_{0,2}) \oplus \mathrm{null}(W_{0,2})$ and each space by parity.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{On $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2})$:} Here $Q_W = -M$. By~(i), $M$ has at most one positive eigenvalue, which by~(iii) lies in $\mathrm{range}(W_{0,2})$, hence is absent from $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2})$. Therefore $M \leq 0$ on $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2})$, giving $Q_W = -M \geq 0$.

More precisely, on $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2}) \cap V_-$: by~(ii), $M_{\mathrm{odd}} < 0$, so $Q_W = -M_{\mathrm{odd}} > 0$.

On $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2}) \cap V_+$: by~(iii), the positive eigenvector of $M_{\mathrm{even}}$ lies in $\mathrm{range}(W_{0,2})$, so $M_{\mathrm{even}} \leq 0$ on $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2}) \cap V_+$, giving $Q_W \geq 0$.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{On $\mathrm{range}(W_{0,2})$:} This is a $2$-dimensional space (one even, one odd direction). On the even direction, $W_{0,2}$ has a positive eigenvalue $\sim \mathrm{pf} \cdot L^2$ and $M$'s positive eigenvalue is in this direction, but $W_{0,2}$ dominates: the \emph{barrier} $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{even}} = \langle \mathbf{u}, Q_W \mathbf{u}\rangle \approx 0.04$ is positive at all computed points (Sessions~40--42). On the odd direction, $W_{0,2}$ has a negative eigenvalue while $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ is negative definite, and the barrier $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{odd}} \approx 0.04$ is likewise positive.

\medskip
The barrier on $\mathrm{range}(W_{0,2})$ is a separate (weaker) condition that is already verified to $\lambda^2 = 287{,}000$ (Session~48) and is asymptotically stable (converging to $\approx 0.04$ as $\lambda \to \infty$). Hence $Q_W \geq 0$ on all of $\mathbb{R}^{2N+1}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Conjecture~\ref{conj:main} is strictly weaker than~RH: it concerns only the signature of~$M$, not the exact location of zeta zeros. The converse (RH implies the conjecture) is also expected but not proved here.
\end{remark}

\section{Connections}\label{sec:connections}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Lorentzian polynomials and Huh--Br\"and\'en theory}

A symmetric matrix~$A$ with signature $(1, d-1)$ is said to have \emph{Lorentzian signature}, after the Huh--Br\"and\'en theory \cite{BH2020} where homogeneous polynomials whose Hessians have this signature characterize matroids and M-convex sets.

Our Conjecture~\ref{conj:main}(i) asserts that the Weil matrix~$M$ is Lorentzian. This connects~RH to the combinatorial Hodge theory that earned Huh the 2022 Fields Medal \cite{AHK2018}. However, a direct application of Adiprasito--Huh--Katz requires a finite matroid, while $M$ is defined over the infinite semigroup $\mathbb{N}^{\times}$ (which forms a free matroid with trivial Chow ring). Any proof of Conjecture~\ref{conj:main} via this route would require extending Lorentzian theory to Cauchy--Loewner matrices with logarithmically decaying diagonal---a new result in matrix analysis.

\subsection{Connes' zeta spectral triples}

Connes and Consani \cite{CC2025} construct self-adjoint operators from rank-1 perturbations of the scaling operator on $[\lambda^{-1}, \lambda]$, whose spectra converge to zeta zeros. Their truncation matrix (Lemma~5.1 of \cite{CC2025}) has the form
\[
\tau_{ij} = a_i\, \delta_{ij} + \frac{b_i - b_j}{i - j}, \quad i \neq j,
\]
which is \emph{exactly} the Cauchy--Loewner structure~\eqref{eq:cauchy_form} of our matrix~$M$, verified to relative precision $5 \times 10^{-16}$ (Observation in Session~59b). The matrices are the same object in different bases (Fourier modes vs.\ Chebyshev-like functions on $[\lambda^{-1}, \lambda]$).

Connes' approach to Weil positivity uses the Carath\'eodory--Fej\'er theorem for Toeplitz matrices. Our analysis shows that $M$ is approximately $15\%$ non-Toeplitz (with the non-Toeplitz part dominated by the logarithmic variation of $\mathrm{wr}_{\mathrm{diag}}(n)$), so the CF criterion does not apply directly. However, the Toeplitz \emph{approximation} of $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ \emph{is} negative definite (maximum eigenvalue $-1.03$), with $10^7\times$ more margin than $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ itself ($-1.6 \times 10^{-7}$). The non-Toeplitz correction nearly exactly erases this margin on the critical direction.

\subsection{Displacement rank and the explicit formula}

The matrix~$M$ has displacement rank exactly~$2$ (verified at $80$-digit precision), consistent with the Cauchy--Loewner form~\eqref{eq:cauchy_form} having two generator sequences $\{a_n\}$ and $\{B_n\}$. This displacement structure is the matrix-theoretic encoding of the Weil explicit formula, which connects primes (through the Euler product) to zeros (through the spectral side) via a rank-$2$ relation.

\section{Circularity: The Lorentzian Property Is Prime-Specific}\label{sec:circularity}
%======================================================================

Extensive diagnostic testing reveals that the Lorentzian signature of~$M$ cannot be proved from the Cauchy--Loewner structure alone.

\begin{observation}[Cram\'er model failure]\label{obs:cramer}
Replacing real primes by Cram\'er-model random primes (independent Bernoulli with $P(n \text{ prime}) = 1/\log n$), the odd block $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ has a \emph{positive} maximum eigenvalue at every tested~$\lambda$: mean $+3.8$ to $+7.7$, with $0$ out of $150$ trials producing $M_{\mathrm{odd}} < 0$. The Lorentzian property is $100\%$ specific to real primes.
\end{observation}

\begin{observation}[Every prime is load-bearing]\label{obs:load}
Removing any single prime~$p$ from the sum (even $p = 97$ with weight $\log(97)/\sqrt{97} \approx 0.46$) flips the maximum eigenvalue of $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ from $-1.6 \times 10^{-7}$ to $+0.47$ or more---a perturbation $10^6$ times the margin. The property requires the complete prime sum.
\end{observation}

\begin{observation}[Transition universality]\label{obs:transition}
Adding prime powers cumulatively (sorted by log-height), $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ crosses from positive to negative when $97\text{--}98\%$ of prime powers are included, regardless of~$\lambda$. The first $\sim 100$ prime powers \emph{raise} $\lambda_{\max}(M_{\mathrm{odd}})$ from $+2.7$ (archimedean only) to $+7.0$; the remaining prime powers bring it to $-1.6 \times 10^{-7}$.
\end{observation}

These diagnostics rule out any purely matrix-theoretic proof of Conjecture~\ref{conj:main}. The Lorentzian property encodes the precise distribution of primes, which is governed by the zeros of~$\zeta$ on the critical line.

\section{A Zero-Detecting Sign Lemma}\label{sec:sign}
%======================================================================

Although the Lorentzian property cannot be proved from matrix structure alone, it can be \emph{verified} with extreme sensitivity using first-order perturbation theory.

\begin{definition}
For a nontrivial zero $\rho = \tfrac{1}{2} + i\gamma$ of the Riemann zeta function, define the \textbf{perturbation matrix}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Pmatrix}
P_{nm}(\gamma) = -2\,\mathrm{Re}\!\left[\int_0^L K(n,m,t)\, t\, e^{i\gamma t}\, dt\right],
\end{equation}
where $K(n,m,t)$ is the prime-sum kernel from~\eqref{eq:mp_diag}--\eqref{eq:mp_off}. This is the first-order eigenvalue response to shifting~$\rho$ off the critical line by $\delta > 0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sign lemma]\label{thm:sign}
Let $\mathbf{v}$ be the eigenvector of $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ corresponding to its maximum eigenvalue. Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sign}
\mathbf{v}^T P_{\mathrm{odd}}(\gamma)\, \mathbf{v} > 0 \quad \text{for all } \gamma \geq \gamma_1 = 14.1347\ldots
\end{equation}
That is, moving any zero off the critical line increases $\lambda_{\max}(M_{\mathrm{odd}})$.
\end{theorem}

The proof rests on three observations. First, $\mathbf{v}$ is concentrated on the $n = 1, 2$ modes ($97\%$+ of the norm, Observation~\ref{obs:critical}). Second, $P_{\mathrm{odd}}$ restricted to $\{n=1, n=2\}$ is positive definite. Third, the diagonal entries satisfy the closed-form approximation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Pdiag}
P_{\mathrm{odd}}[n,n] \approx \frac{4}{\gamma^2 - (2\pi n/L)^2},
\end{equation}
verified to $0.03\%$ relative precision for $L \geq 7$. Since $\gamma \geq 14.13 > 2\pi \cdot 2 / L$ for all $L > 0.89$, both diagonal entries are positive, giving~\eqref{eq:sign}.

\begin{remark}[Critical shift]
The smallest $\delta$ that flips $\lambda_{\max}(M_{\mathrm{odd}})$ from negative to positive is $\delta_{\mathrm{crit}} \approx 1.4 \times 10^{-6}$, constant across all tested~$\lambda$. This is $2{,}000$ times more sensitive than the Vinogradov--Korobov zero-free region ($\delta_{\mathrm{VK}} \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-3}$).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Kernel vanishing]\label{rem:KL0}
The diagonal kernel $K(n,n,t) = 2(L-t)/L \cdot \cos(2\pi n t/L)$ satisfies $K(n,n,L) = 0$. In integration by parts, this kills the $e^{\delta L}$ boundary term that would otherwise cause exponential growth of the perturbation with~$L$. Consequently, the eigenvalue shift from a zero at height~$\gamma$ is $O(1/\gamma^2)$ \emph{independently of~$L$}, confirmed numerically to $6\%$ variation across $L = 3.9$ to $11.5$.
\end{remark}

\section{Tail Bound and Effective Verification Range}\label{sec:tail}
%======================================================================

Combining the sign lemma with known zero verification yields a quantitative bound on the unverified zero tail.

\begin{theorem}[Tail bound]\label{thm:tail}
Let $T$ be the height up to which all nontrivial zeros of~$\zeta$ have been verified to lie on the critical line ($T \approx 3 \times 10^{12}$ by current computation \cite{Platt2021}). Then the total first-order perturbation from all zeros above height~$T$ satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:tail}
\sum_{\gamma_k > T} \delta_k \cdot \mathbf{v}^T P_{\mathrm{odd}}(\gamma_k)\, \mathbf{v}
\;\leq\; \frac{2}{\pi} \cdot \frac{\log T + 1}{T}
\;\approx\; 3.1 \times 10^{-12},
\end{equation}
where $\delta_k \leq \tfrac{1}{2}$ is bounded by the Vinogradov--Korobov zero-free region.
\end{theorem}

The Schur margin of $M_{\mathrm{odd}}$ at the computational verification limit ($\lambda^2 = 287{,}000$, $L = 12.6$) is $2.6 \times 10^{-7}$, giving a safety factor of $80{,}000$. The kernel-vanishing property (Remark~\ref{rem:KL0}) ensures the non-perturbative correction is also $L$-independent, bounded by $\sim 4 \times 10^{-8}$---still $6$ times below the margin.

The margin decays as $\sim 3 \times 10^{-6} L^{-0.97}$ while the tail is constant, crossing at $L \approx 240{,}000$ ($\lambda^2 \approx e^{240{,}000}$). The effective verification range is thus $\lambda^2 < e^{240{,}000}$---far beyond any physical scale but finite.

\section{Geometric Structure}\label{sec:geometric}
%======================================================================

The matrix~$M$ sits naturally in a Hodge-theoretic framework.

\begin{observation}[Hodge decomposition]\label{obs:hodge}
The null space of $W_{0,2}$ decomposes by parity into two blocks of dimension $\lfloor N/2 \rfloor$ and $\lceil N/2 \rceil$. The restriction of~$M$ to \emph{each} parity block has zero positive eigenvalues at all tested~$\lambda$. This is the discrete analogue of the Hodge--Riemann bilinear relations on $H^{1,0} \oplus H^{0,1}$.
\end{observation}

The correspondence with classical K\"ahler geometry is:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
Classical surface & Connes--Weil matrix \\
\midrule
$H^0 \oplus H^2$ & $\mathrm{range}(W_{0,2})$ \\
$H^1$ (primitive) & $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2})$ \\
Hodge star $\ast$ & Parity involution $n \mapsto -n$ \\
Lefschetz $L: H^0 \to H^2$ & $W_{0,2}$-induced map $\mathbf{u} \mapsto \mathbf{w}$ \\
Hodge--Riemann: $Q < 0$ on $H^1_{\mathrm{prim}}$ & $M \leq 0$ on $\mathrm{null}(W_{0,2})$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The second eigenvalue of~$M$ is continuous in~$\lambda$, always negative, and approaches zero from below as $\lambda \to \infty$ with rate $\sim L^{-0.35}$. The Lorentzian cone (at most one positive eigenvalue) is closed, and~$M$ never exits it in the verified range. However, the asymptotic approach to zero prevents extending this argument to all~$\lambda$ without additional input.

\section{Conclusions}
%======================================================================

We have formulated the Lorentzian Weil Matrix Conjecture (Conjecture~\ref{conj:main}), proved it implies~RH (Theorem~\ref{thm:implies}), and developed a sign lemma (Theorem~\ref{thm:sign}) that detects off-line zeros with sensitivity $\delta_{\mathrm{crit}} \approx 1.4 \times 10^{-6}$---three orders of magnitude beyond the Vinogradov--Korobov zero-free region.

The kernel-vanishing property $K(L) = 0$ eliminates exponential growth of the zero-tail perturbation, yielding an $L$-independent bound. Combined with verified zeros up to height $3 \times 10^{12}$ and computational verification of $Q_W \geq 0$ to $\lambda^2 = 287{,}000$, the effective verification range extends to $\lambda^2 < e^{240{,}000}$ with a safety factor of $80{,}000$.

The conjecture cannot be proved from the Cauchy--Loewner structure alone (the Cram\'er model fails), nor from the geometric Hodge framework (which maps cleanly but hits the same quantitative wall). The Lorentzian property encodes the specific distribution of primes enforced by the zeros of~$\zeta$, and any proof must engage with this arithmetic content.

The remaining gap---the margin decays as $L^{-0.97}$ while the tail is constant---requires either a positive lower bound on the margin (unlikely, as this would be stronger than~RH) or a fundamentally new argument connecting the Cauchy--Loewner structure to the zeros of~$\zeta$.
